Short Bio

Joe Davis (ARSM) is a 23-year-old Jamaican-British musical director, arranger, and multi-instrumentalist based in Kingston, Jamaica. At 15, he became the first person in Jamaica to receive a distinction in the ARSM Classical Piano Diploma exam. He attended Idyllwild Arts Academy in California — graduating as "Most Outstanding Artist" — and later enrolled at Berklee College of Music in Boston, studying Contemporary Writing & Production.

His credits include co-arranging GloRilla's Saturday Night Live performance, producing and arranging for Kirk Franklin's "Church Music," performing as bassist and co-arranger for RES at the Atlanta Peach Drop, and serving as MD for Tessellated, Verlando Small, and others. He was personally invited to DJ Jazzy Jeff's PLAYLIST Retreat twice and was featured in Rolling Stone.

Proficient on eight instruments — Vocals, Drums, Keys, Bass, Guitar, Steel Pan, Melodica, and Mixed Percussion — Joe brings rare versatility to every engagement. He is recognised by Jacob Collier, Questlove, Tori Kelly, DJ Jazzy Jeff, Shontelle, and others. He co-founded The Garden JA and Chromatics Creative Studios, and holds dual Jamaican-British citizenship. Available for international engagements worldwide.

Extended Bio

Born in England and raised in Jamaica from the age of 10, Joe Davis (ARSM) carries two national identities with equal pride — and both are audible in his music. His musical journey began with classical piano at age 5, progressing rapidly through formal exams and self-taught transcription by ear. By 15 he had made history as the first — and at the time only — person in Jamaica to receive a distinction in the ARSM Classical Piano Diploma exam.

He went on to Idyllwild Arts Academy in California, where he majored in Jazz Piano and minored in Songwriting, graduating as the school's "Most Outstanding Artist." He then enrolled at Berklee College of Music in Boston, studying Contemporary Writing & Production, before returning to Jamaica to build his career on home turf. His academic work has been paralleled by an extraordinary performance record — including headlining a sold-out show at the Berklee Performance Center, performing at SXSW with Tessellated and Jaz Elise, and presenting the Star-Spangled Banner at a ceremony honouring Vice President Kamala Harris.

Joe's arranging and production credits read like a who's-who of contemporary gospel, R&B, and Caribbean music. He has arranged for Kirk Franklin, GloRilla (Saturday Night Live), Mary Mary, Karen Clark-Sheard, RES, Tessellated, King Cruff, Lyric Jones, and Melba Moore, among many others. As a live performer, he has shared stages with Lila Iké, Elephant Man, Sevana, DJ Jazzy Jeff, Brayla, and performed at Jamaica Jazz & Blues Festival, WOMEX, the Atlantic Peach Drop, and the Island Music Conference. His radio credits span BBC 1Xtra (SeaniB), Jamaica's Nationwide 90, Fyah 105, and Kool 97.

He has been personally invited to DJ Jazzy Jeff's exclusive PLAYLIST Retreat twice — collaborating with De La Soul, Kaelin Ellis, Steve McKie, The Pocket Queen, Yaahn Hunter, and others — and was featured in Rolling Stone magazine as a result. His online presence spans over 3 million views and 30,000+ followers across Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok, with recognition from Jacob Collier, Questlove, Tori Kelly, Tyla, Willow Smith, Masego, Mahalia, Kevin Olusola, and DJ Jazzy Jeff.

Beyond performance and arranging, Joe co-founded The Garden JA — a platform dedicated to showcasing Jamaica's finest young musicians — and Chromatics Creative Studios, a visual media company specialising in photography, videography, and graphic design. He is also developing The Musician's Digital Encore, a course aimed at helping musicians navigate the digital landscape.

Joe is also a chromesthete — he perceives music as vivid colour, a form of synesthesia that gives his arrangements a visual logic and emotional precision that distinguishes his work. He is a man of faith, a breakdancer, a programmer, and a firm believer that the best music rooms also laugh the most.
